Fritada

Traditional Ecuadorian dish made with braised pork, typically served with potato tortillas, boiled potatoes, cooked corn, pickled onions, and fried plantains. The pork is cooked in a flavorful liquid, then fried in its own rendered fat until browned and caramelized.

Ingredients

  • Pork: Typically pork ribs or loin, cut into squares
  • Water: Used for boiling the pork initially
  • Orange Juice: Adds a tangy sweetness and helps with browning
  • Onion: Chopped or sliced, adds depth of flavor
  • Garlic: Minced or whole cloves, for aromatics
  • Cumin: A key spice for flavor
  • Salt and Pepper: For seasoning
  • Optional additions:
    • Llapingachos (potato patties), boiled yuca, mote (hominy), or fried ripe plantains for serving
    • Aji (hot sauce) for those who like heat
    • Curtido (pickled onions and tomatoes)

Steps

  • Prepare the Pork: Cut the pork into desired pieces and season with salt, pepper, and cumin
  • Marinate (Optional): If time allows, marinate the pork for a couple of hours to allow the flavors to penetrate
  • Cook the Pork: Combine the pork, water, orange juice, onion, and garlic in a large pan or wok
  • Braise: Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the liquid reduces and the pork starts to brown
  • Reduce and Brown: Continue cooking, stirring frequently to prevent burning, until the pork is evenly browned and tender
  • Serve: Enjoy the fritada with your chosen side dishes
